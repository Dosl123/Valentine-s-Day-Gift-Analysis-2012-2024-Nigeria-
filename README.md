# Valentine’s Day Gift Analysis (2012–2024, Nigeria)
Valentine’s Day gift analysis (2012–2024, Nigeria) exploring self-gifting trends, popular products, and retailer performance. Built using SQL for data creation and Excel for analysis and visualization to uncover consumer behavior insights.

### Overview
Valentine’s Day in Nigeria is celebrated with great enthusiasm, marked by a significant increase in gift exchanges and retail activity compared to other holidays. This project explores gifting trends over time, with a unique focus on individuals who purchase gifts for themselves as a form of self-expression and celebration.

This dataset was created by me, Oluwafisayomi Christabel Dosunmu. I designed the database, inserted the data using SQL, and performed analysis and visualization using Excel.

---

### Dataset Description
The dataset includes the following fields:
- Customer ID  
- Customer Name  
- Category  
- Self-Gift Indicator  
- Online Retailer  
- Price  
- Purchase Date  
- Delivery Date

### Key Analysis Areas
- Top recipients of self-gifts  
- Most popular online retailers  
- Most purchased gift categories  
- Trends in self-gifting over time  

---

### Key Insights

#### Top Self-Gift Recipient
Analysis showed that the top recipient of self-gifts over the years was an individual who made a high-value purchase (a car) on February 2, 2024.

#### Most Popular Gifts
Rings and flowers emerged as the most frequently purchased gifts, each appearing multiple times across the years.

#### Self-Gifting Trends by Gender
A higher proportion of self-gifting was observed among females, representing a significant share of participants.

#### Top Performing Retailer
One retailer stood out as the leading platform for gift purchases, driven by the availability of affordable and popular items such as perfumes, flowers, and accessories.

#### Trend Evolution
Self-gifting behavior evolved over time, with fluctuations in participation and gift preferences. Initially driven by individuals, the trend gradually expanded to include couples and younger participants.

---

### Conclusion
The analysis highlights the growing culture of self-gifting during Valentine’s Day in Nigeria. Over time, participation has increased, and the trend continues to evolve, reflecting changing consumer behavior and social influence.
